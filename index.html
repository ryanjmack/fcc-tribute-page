Vincent van Gogh

Dutch post-impressionist painter

Short Biography

Vincent van Gogh would become one of the most well-known artists in the world. His paintings have become easily recognizable to cultures throughout the world, and he has become the archetypal “tortured artist.” After just ten years of painting and producing some 900 paintings, Vincent van Gogh took his own life in 1890. Never fully appreciated in his own time, it wouldn’t take long for the art world to recognize the genius they lost. Within twenty years of his death, there were memorial shows of his works all over the world – influencing generations of artists to come.

  • Born on March 30, 1853, in Groot-Zundert, Netherlands
  • 1880 - 1884 Van Gogh relocates to Brussels and begins learning the skills needed to become an artist
  • 1886 - March - Van Gogh moves to Paris. Discovers Impressionism and Post Impressionism
  • 1888 - Van Gogh begins suffering from mental problems
  • 1888 - December - Van Gogh cuts off a portion of his ear and commits himself to a mental asylum in Saint Rémy
  • 1890 - July 29 - Vincent Van Gogh dies of a self-inflicted gunshot. He was buried on July 30 at Auvers-sur-Oise
